WHAT THE DUCK

Fun Facts

  • Ducks have excellent vision, thanks to the shape of their eyes, they can see objects both near and far simultaneously. To top it off, ducks have three eyelids and can see in color.
  • Ducks are capable of sensing threats in the environment in under a second.
  • Because a duck's eyes are located on either side of its head, they have a field of vision of nearly 340 degrees.
  • Preening is the process by which ducks groom themselves—getting rid of dust, dirt, and parasites from their feathers, while also helping to waterproof their outer layer. During preening, ducks spread a waxy, waterproof oil secreted by their uropygial gland, which is located near their tails.
  • After a male duck, or drake's, chosen mate is hatching eggs,the birds undergo molting, temporarily losing their bright plumage, as well as their ability to fly.
  • The more daylight there is, the more eggs a hen produces. Farmers who raise ducks will often turn to artificial lighting in order to give their hens about 17 hours of light a day.
  • The omnivorous birds also tend to consume gravel, small stones, or sand—not for the nutritional value, but so they can store the substances in their gizzards and use the rough textures to break down food.
  • Very rarely, a severe weather event will trigger a mass migration known as a “grand passage”. There have been only three recorded instances of grand passages: one in 1940, one in 1955, and the most recent in 1995, when a reported 90 million waterfowl migrated from Canada after a severe cold front set in, causing major problems at airports along the birds' route.
  • Unlike the young of many other animals, ducklings achieve independence almost immediately after hatching.
  • Their webbed feet have no nerves or blood vessels, rendering them incapable of feeling the cold. Which is an important adaptation when you consider that ducks can be found on every continent except for the inhospitable Antarctica.
  • Ducks can live longer than dogs, Desi the Duck, a female mallard who lived in the United Kingdom, was 20 years old when she died, making her the world’s oldest duck ever. The average lifespan of a duck is between 5-10 years, but domesticated ducks can live into their teens.

Famous Ducks

Daffy Duck

Daffy Duck is an animated cartoon character produced by Warner Bros. Styled as an anthropomorphic black duck, the character has appeared in cartoon series such as Looney Tunes and Merrie Melodies, in which he usually has been depicted as a foil for Bugs Bunny.

Donald Duck

Donald Duck is an animated character created by Walt Disney. He is a hot-headed, anthropomorphic duck that is often the victim of exceptionally bad luck. Normally depicted wearing a sailor shirt, cap, and a bow tie—but no trousers at all.

Scrooge McDuck

Scrooge is an elderly Scottish anthropomorphic Pekin duck with a yellow-orange bill, legs, and feet. He typically wears a red or blue frock coat, top hat, pince-nez glasses, and spats. He is portrayed in animations as speaking with a Scottish accent.

Aflac Duck

The Aflac Duck has been the official mascot of American insurance company Aflac since 1999 and Aflac Japan since 2003. He is an American Pekin duck known for frustratedly quacking Aflac's name to unsuspecting policy holders.

Howard the Duck

Howard the Duck is an English-speaking, human child-sized, anthropomorphic duck that the late Steve Gerber and artist Val Mayerik created in issue #19 of the Marvel comic Adventure into Fear (1973). According to Marvel, he's the eldest son of Dave and Dottie and was a continuous source of disappointment for them, because he had trouble holding a steady job, and didn't fit into society on his home planet of Duckworld.
